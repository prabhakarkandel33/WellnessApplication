CAFFEINE SLEEP IMPACT

What is caffeine sleep impact:
Caffeine is a stimulant that can delay sleep onset, reduce total sleep time, and decrease deep sleep.

What to do:
1. Avoid caffeine at least 6 hours before bedtime.  
2. Be aware of hidden caffeine in tea, chocolate, and energy drinks.  
3. Limit daily caffeine intake to moderate amounts.  
4. Substitute with herbal teas or decaf in the evening.

What NOT to do:
- Consume caffeine late in the day.  
- Rely on caffeine as a substitute for sleep.

Effects of excess caffeine:
- Difficulty falling asleep.  
- Poor sleep quality.  
- Daytime fatigue and irritability.
