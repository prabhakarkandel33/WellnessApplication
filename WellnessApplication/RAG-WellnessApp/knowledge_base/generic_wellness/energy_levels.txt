ENERGY LEVELS

What affects energy levels:
Energy levels are influenced by sleep, nutrition, physical activity, hydration, stress, and overall health. Low energy can result from fatigue, poor diet, dehydration, or medical conditions.

How to boost energy:
1. Get enough quality sleep each night.  
2. Eat balanced meals with complex carbs, proteins, and healthy fats.  
3. Stay hydrated throughout the day.  
4. Engage in regular physical activity.  
5. Manage stress with relaxation techniques or mindfulness.  

What NOT to do:
- Rely on excessive caffeine or energy drinks.  
- Skip meals or overeat junk food.  
- Ignore persistent fatigue, which may indicate health issues.  

When to see a doctor:
- Chronic low energy or extreme fatigue  
- Shortness of breath, dizziness, or palpitations  
- Sudden unexplained changes in energy  

Benefits of maintaining energy:
- Improved focus and productivity  
- Better mood and mental clarity  
- Enhanced physical performance  
- Overall well-being
