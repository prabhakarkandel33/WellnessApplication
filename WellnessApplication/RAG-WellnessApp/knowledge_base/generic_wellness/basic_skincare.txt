BASIC SKINCARE

What is basic skincare:
Basic skincare is the routine care to keep skin clean, hydrated, and healthy. It helps prevent dryness, acne, and premature aging.

Daily routine:
1. Cleanse – wash face with gentle cleanser morning and night.
2. Moisturize – apply moisturizer suitable for your skin type.
3. Sunscreen – use SPF 30 or higher during the day, even indoors.
4. Hydrate – drink water to keep skin healthy.
5. Optional – gentle exfoliation 1-2 times a week to remove dead skin cells.

What NOT to do:
- Overwash or scrub harshly.
- Skip sunscreen.
- Use too many products at once.
- Pop pimples (can cause scarring).

When to see a dermatologist:
- Persistent acne, rashes, or irritation.
- Sudden changes in moles or spots.
- Skin infections or unusual growths.

Prevention:
- Follow consistent daily routine.
- Eat balanced diet.
- Manage stress and get enough sleep.
- Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol.

Recovery time:
Benefits show in days to weeks; skin health improves with consistency over months.
