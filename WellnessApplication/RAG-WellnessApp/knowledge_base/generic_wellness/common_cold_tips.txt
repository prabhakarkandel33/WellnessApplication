COMMON COLD TIPS

What is a common cold:
A common cold is a viral infection of the upper respiratory tract. Symptoms include runny nose, sneezing, sore throat, cough, mild fever, and fatigue.

What to do:
1. Rest – allow your body to recover.  
2. Hydrate – drink water, herbal teas, and clear fluids.  
3. Eat light, nutritious foods – soups, fruits, and vegetables.  
4. Relieve symptoms – over-the-counter decongestants, throat lozenges, or pain relievers if needed.  
5. Humidify – use a humidifier or inhale steam to ease congestion.  

What NOT to do:
- Ignore severe symptoms or high fever.  
- Smoke or be around smoke.  
- Overuse antibiotics (not effective against viruses).  

When to see a doctor:
- Persistent high fever  
- Shortness of breath or chest pain  
- Symptoms lasting more than 10 days or worsening  
- Severe sinus or ear pain  

Prevention:
- Wash hands frequently  
- Avoid close contact with sick people  
- Cover mouth and nose when sneezing or coughing  
- Maintain healthy diet and sleep  

Recovery time:
Usually 7–10 days; symptoms often improve gradually.
