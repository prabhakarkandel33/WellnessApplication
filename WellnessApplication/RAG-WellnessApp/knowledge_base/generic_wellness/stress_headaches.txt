STRESS HEADACHES

What is a stress headache:
Stress headaches, also called tension headaches, are caused by muscle tension, stress, or anxiety. Symptoms include dull, aching head pain, tightness around the forehead or neck, and sensitivity to light or sound.

What to do:
1. Rest in a quiet, dark room.  
2. Hydrate – drink water regularly.  
3. Apply cold or warm compress – cold for pain, warm for muscle tension.  
4. Relaxation techniques – deep breathing, meditation, or gentle stretching.  
5. Over-the-counter pain relief – ibuprofen or acetaminophen if needed.  

What NOT to do:
- Skip meals or go hungry.  
- Overuse painkillers (can cause rebound headaches).  
- Ignore severe or sudden headaches.  

When to see a doctor:
- Headache after head injury  
- Sudden severe headache or “worst headache of your life”  
- Persistent headaches not relieved by usual methods  
- Vision changes, weakness, numbness, or slurred speech  

Prevention:
- Manage stress with regular relaxation or exercise  
- Maintain proper posture  
- Stay hydrated and eat regular meals  
- Limit caffeine and alcohol  

Recovery time:
Usually a few hours to a day; persistent or severe headaches require medical attention.
