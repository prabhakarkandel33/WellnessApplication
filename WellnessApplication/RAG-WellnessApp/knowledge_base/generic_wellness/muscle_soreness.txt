MUSCLE SORENESS

What is muscle soreness:
Muscle soreness is discomfort or stiffness in muscles, often after exercise or physical activity. Common types are delayed onset muscle soreness (DOMS) and acute soreness.

What to do:
1. Rest – allow muscles time to recover.  
2. Gentle stretching – helps relieve stiffness.  
3. Hydrate – water and electrolytes aid recovery.  
4. Light activity – walking or low-intensity movement can improve circulation.  
5. Pain relief – over-the-counter options like ibuprofen if needed.  
6. Massage or foam rolling – can reduce tension and soreness.  

What NOT to do:
- Overstrain sore muscles.  
- Ignore severe or sharp pain.  

When to see a doctor:
- Swelling, bruising, or severe pain.  
- Muscle weakness or inability to move normally.  
- Persistent soreness lasting more than a week.  

Prevention:
- Warm up before exercise.  
- Gradually increase intensity.  
- Cool down and stretch after workouts.  
- Maintain proper hydration and nutrition.  

Recovery time:
Usually 1–3 days depending on intensity and type of activity.
