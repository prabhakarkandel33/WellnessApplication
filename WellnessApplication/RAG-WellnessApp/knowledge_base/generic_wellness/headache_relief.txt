HEADACHE RELIEF

What is a headache:
A headache is pain or discomfort in the head or scalp. Common types include tension headaches, migraines, and cluster headaches. Symptoms may include throbbing pain, pressure, sensitivity to light or sound, nausea.

What to do:
1. Drink water – dehydration can cause headaches.
2. Rest in a quiet, dark room.
3. Pain relief – over-the-counter medications like ibuprofen or acetaminophen.
4. Cold or warm compress – cold for migraines, warm for tension headaches.
5. Caffeine in moderation – small amounts can help.
6. Relaxation techniques – deep breathing, meditation, gentle stretching.

What NOT to do:
- Skip meals.
- Overuse painkillers (can cause rebound headaches).
- Ignore severe or sudden headaches.

When to see a doctor:
- Headache after head injury.
- Sudden, severe “worst headache of your life.”
- Vision changes, slurred speech, weakness, numbness.
- Persistent headaches not responding to usual remedies.

Prevention:
- Stay hydrated.
- Eat regular meals.
- Maintain good posture.
- Manage stress.
- Limit alcohol and caffeine.

Recovery time:
Usually a few hours to a day depending on type and severity. Persistent or severe headaches require medical attention.
